Wednesday, September 7

The most important thing I learned this weekend has to do with electrical current. From the Petzold, I learned that current is equal to the voltage divided by resistance. Current is measured in amps, voltage in volts, and resistance in ohms. This relationship can be represented with the equality "I = V ÷ Ω," where "I" represents current in amps, "V" represents voltage in volts, and "Ω" represents resistance in ohms. Current here represents the sheer number of electrons flowing past a given point, and I recall that one amp was equivalent to some mind bogglingly large number of electrons. From current, we can find the power in watts by simply multiplying the voltage times the current, as in "A = V × I."
What is amazing to me about all of this is that I definitely studied this at some point in one of the two physics classes I took in high school, but for whatever reason, it never stuck. I find it so interesting that a concept that I have had a hard time understanding for so many years took one morning with a good explanation and proper study habits to firmly ingraine. I suspect that this project of systematic study of machine learning will also fill in a lot of the basic gaps left by my education, and I suspect that this improved comprehension will be useful far outside of this context!
